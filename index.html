WHAT IS A WHOLESALER

Wholesale is the sale of goods in large quantities, typically to retailers or other businesses rather than individual consumers. It serves as a link between manufacturers and retailers, enabling efficient distribution and reducing costs through bulk transactions.

April 22, 2017

FUNCTIONS of WHOLESALER:

The main functions of a wholesaler include buying goods in bulk, storing them, and supplying them to retailers in smaller quantities. Wholesalers also help in price stabilization, reduce the burden of inventory for producers, and facilitate smooth distribution within the supply chain.

April 18, 2017

CHARACTERISTICS:

Wholesalers typically buy in bulk, sell to retailers or businesses, and do not deal directly with end consumers. They maintain large inventories, offer storage and transportation, and often provide credit and market information to retailers.

CLASSIFICATION
OF WHOLESALE TRADE

Wholesale trade is classified into merchant wholesalers and agents/brokers. Merchant wholesalers buy and resell goods, taking ownership. Agents and brokers arrange sales between buyers and sellers without taking ownership, earning a commission.

VS RETAILERS

    Location – Wholesalers operate from warehouses | Retailers operate from shops.
